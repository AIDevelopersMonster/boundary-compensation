## Central publication thesis

The exact curved-volume target is a functional of the full registered holonomy/character datum and cannot be reduced to one local cubic scalar. Finite-level categorical and skein-compatible candidates therefore require an explicit ordering audit. The resulting error is most effectively controlled not by label symmetry or target harmonic complexity alone, but by an operator-adapted residual-frame inequality. Prospective level/family tests show family-dependent crossover of lower-symbol visibility; matched-shape tests isolate an absolute-ray-scale effect, while the two-parameter audit shows that clean separation of representation resolution from quantum-group deformation is not identifiable in the current parameterization.

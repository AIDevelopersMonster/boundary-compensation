Title:
Boundary Compensation Origin VI: Spectral Admissibility Collapse and Projection Failure in Frustrated Shadow-Gauge Ensembles

Subtitle:

Description:
This record contains the sixth technical manuscript in the Boundary Compensation Origin program. The paper studies spectral admissibility collapse in finite frustrated shadow-gauge ensembles. Building on BC-Origin V, holonomy configurations are sampled from a finite simplicial Z2 shadow-gauge ensemble rather than from an externally imposed random-sign ratio. For each holonomy configuration, the shadow-readout operator is constructed and its lowest spectral margin is compared with a finite-resolution threshold. Projection failure is defined as loss of local readout admissibility, not as physical particle annihilation or empirical vacuum dynamics. The package includes the manuscript, LaTeX source, Python software, generated figures, a browser-based HTML lab, bibliographic files, and descriptions without formulas.

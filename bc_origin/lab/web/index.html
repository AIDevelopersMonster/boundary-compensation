Signed operator

Interpretation: same orientation raises inverse-scale denominators and compresses shadows. Opposite orientation lowers denominators and may push a branch to the admissibility horizon.

What the program demonstrates

This lab visualizes the BC-Origin I toy model. Hidden oriented winding labels determine an interaction sign, and the signed spectral operator shows localization, delocalization, and admissibility-horizon crossing.

The program is not a physical simulator, does not extract real constants, and does not prove a physical hidden-sector model. It makes the algebraic mechanism of the article inspectable.

A localized shadow requires positive eigen-denominators. When the lower branch crosses zero, the scale ratio diverges and the branch leaves the localized interpretation of the toy model.

Symbols and sliders

  • n1, n2: hidden oriented winding labels.
  • s = sign(n1*n2): orientation product.
  • gamma: strength of the signed diagonal shift.
  • kappa: off-diagonal coupling that separates the two spectral branches.
  • d1, d2: baseline inverse-scale denominators.
  • mu: effective scale multiplier for ell/L.
  • lambda minus / lambda plus: eigen-denominators of D_signed.
  • ell/L: observable scale ratio, defined only for positive lambda.

Characteristic parameter points

These buttons do not add new claims. They only place the toy model in regimes where its basic effects are easy to inspect.

Model: D = [[d1 + gamma*s, kappa], [kappa, d2 + gamma*s]], s = sign(n1*n2). Localized shadow requires lambda > 0.

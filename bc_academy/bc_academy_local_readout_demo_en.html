BC-Academy I: Local Readout Channel

Interactive companion to the optional applied lecture: projectors, local excitation, readout switching, and an induced gap.

Safety note. This demonstration is not a strict physical model of the electron, time, velocity, or vacuum. It visualizes one teaching intuition: how finite-dimensional spectral algebra may serve as a language for local finite-resolution readout.

3. Motion as switching of readout statuses

One active local channel moves across the grid. This is not a physical trajectory. It is a visual intuition: a macroscopic observer may read a sequence of switches as motion.

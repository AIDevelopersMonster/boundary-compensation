## 1. Exact reduction

Let

\[
B_E=\frac1{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
B_G(\theta)=F_J(\theta)B_EF_J(\theta)^T,
\]

where `F_J(theta)` is a real orthogonal two-channel recoupling matrix. Define

\[
c_J(\theta)=\langle B_E,B_G(\theta)\rangle_{HS}.
\]

If `C_J(theta)` contains the coordinates of `B_E` and `B_G` in an orthonormal basis of `Sym_0(2,R)`, then

\[
C_J^TC_J=
\begin{pmatrix}
1&c_J\\c_J&1
\end{pmatrix}
\]

and consequently

\[
\boxed{\sigma_{\min}(C_J)^2=1-|c_J|}.
\]

Moreover,

\[
\|[B_E,B_G]\|_{HS}^2=2(1-c_J^2).
\]

Thus abstract basis collapse is equivalent to projective coincidence of the two contrasts.

For

\[
F_J=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix},
\]

orthogonality gives

\[
c_J=2a^2-1.
\]

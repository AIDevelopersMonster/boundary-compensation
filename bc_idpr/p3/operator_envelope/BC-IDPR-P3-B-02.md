## 1. Scientific purpose

P3-B-M1 established a nonzero nuisance-quotient tangent in a closed two-channel formula at the Ising anchor. M2 tests whether this survives four simultaneous strengthenings:

1. the recoupling matrix is evaluated directly from a finite q-Racah sum;

4. nontriviality is checked on a compact regular interval, not only at one point.

\[

\[

## 9. Exact continuum nontriviality certificate

The squared centered cubic spectral invariant

\[
\mathcal R(\theta)
=
\frac{\bigl(\operatorname{Tr}X_c^3\bigr)^2}
{\bigl(\operatorname{Tr}X_c^2\bigr)^3}
\]

is invariant under independent affine changes of `X` and under conjugation.

With

\[
x=\cos2\theta,
\]

it reduces exactly to

\[
\mathcal R(x)
=
\frac{(x+1)^2(2x-1)^2(4x+1)^2}
{6(4x^2+2x+1)^3}.
\]

Its derivative is

\[
\frac{d\mathcal R}{d\theta}
=
-\frac{
18\sin2\theta\;x(x+1)(2x-1)(2x+1)(4x+1)
}{(4x^2+2x+1)^4}.
\]

For

\[
\theta\in I,
\qquad
x=\cos2\theta>\frac12,
\]

all factors apart from the leading minus sign are positive. Therefore

\[
\frac{d\mathcal R}{d\theta}<0
\quad\text{on all of }I.
\]

This proves continuum nontriviality modulo the declared independent affine and conjugation nuisances. The proof is separate from the sampled Hilbert--Schmidt margin.
